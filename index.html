Intro

I hold a MSc in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Manitoba, where my dissertation focused on attitude control of an underactuated LEO CubeSat in the presence of uncertainties using state estimation. I also completed a MSc in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Tehran, where my dissertation focused on the design of optimal robust fuzzy and sliding mode controllers for the attitude control of geostationary satellites using statistical optimization analysis. I received my BSc in Aerospace Engineering from the Amirkabir University of Technology (Polytechnic of Tehran), where my dissertation involved the analysis of the main systems of a GE-CF6-80A3 turbofan engine. I'm originally from Iran, but am currently living in Canada. By the way, check out my resume.

About

I have a strong passion for using my expertise and skills to contribute to the advancement of space technology and exploration. In my professional experience, I have served as the ADCS Lead for a space mission, where I was responsible for the design and implementation of the attitude determination and control subsystem. I am currently a member of the UMSATS team, where I am involved in the analysis and simulation of spacecraft orbits and the design of guidance, navigation, and control algorithms. I have also held internships as an Aerospace Engineer and a Structural Engineer, where I gained hands-on experience in the design and analysis of aircraft and spacecraft operational and support hazard analysis (OSHA).

In addition to my technical skills, I am highly skilled in technical documentation and presentation, written and oral communication, and proactive problem-solving. I have received multiple awards and scholarships, including a scholarship from AWS to learn machine learning.

As a progressive engineer with over 5 years of experience in Aerospace engineering, particularly in control systems and attitude control design, I have a strong background in spacecraft dynamics modeling, Kalman filter, simulation, and verification using MATLAB and Simulink. I also have in-depth knowledge in space mission geometry modeling and simulation. In terms of computer proficiencies, I have expertise in a variety of programming languages and software including Python, MATLAB, Simulink, AutoCAD, C#, Unity3D, STK (Systems Tool Kit), EES (Engineering Equation Software), ABAQUS, ANSYS, AAA (Advanced Aircraft Analysis Software), and Digital Datcom. I also have experience in the development of Arduino-based embedded systems.

Sponsorship from Github would be extremely important in helping me to continue my volunteer activities with IEEE Winnipeg, as well as allowing me to continue working on my software for satellite attitude control. The funds would cover the costs associated with these endeavors, such as purchasing necessary equipment and software, and allowing me to dedicate more time and resources to these projects.
